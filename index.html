Prioritize initiatives by Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort. Higher score = better bang for the buck. Rows re-rank as you type.

    Mode 04 — About

    About this Atlas

    A field guide to deciding well — 23 decision-making frameworks matched to the decision in front of you, explained in plain terms, and built into tools you can actually use.

    The idea

    There's no single "best" decision framework — only a best fit for the situation. This Atlas is organized around the two factors that determine fit most: how reversible a decision is, and how knowable the situation is. Reversible, clear calls deserve fast and cheap methods; irreversible, uncertain ones deserve slow, bias-resistant ones. The most common mistake is treating an easily-reversed decision with the heavy process only a permanent one warrants — that's how people and teams get slow.

    The three working modes

    Match — answer up to four questions; the map lights up the right region and ranks your best-fit frameworks, breaking ties toward the most purpose-built one.

    Library — all 23 frameworks, filterable by domain or trait, each with how to run it, an example, and a watch-out.

    Tools — four working templates: a weighted decision matrix, RICE calculator, Eisenhower sorter, and pre-mortem worksheet, each with a copy button.
